Racing Game Engine
University project where I worked together with 5 other programmers to create a racing game engine. I was responsible for the AI behaviour of the cars in the scene. The way the AI 'sees' where it is located on the track, should it slow down or speed up, should it make a turn, everything managed through track nodes scattered around the track. The engine runs on both PC and SteamDeck. The game engine was made in 8 weeks.
C++ Custom Engine Physics & AI Small Team PC & SteamDeck
Couzin's Model Flocking Behaviour
University project where I researched physically accurate flocking behaviour in the form of Couzin's Model. Each agent avoids its direct neighbours, aligns with its close neighbours, and moves towards further away neighbours. All coming together to exhibit flocking behaviour. At the end of the 8 week project, I wrote a blog post showcasing my results: https://dev.to/kschuilenburg/simulating-realistic-group-behaviour-using-couzins-model-in-c-152g
